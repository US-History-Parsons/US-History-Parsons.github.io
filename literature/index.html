Literature and Culture During the Great Depression



The Perspective of Mice and Men

The novel shows life as unstable and uncertain. Characters move constantly for work, with little chance of long-term security. The dream of owning land represents hope but also how unreachable stability was.

Povert, Migration, Labor, and Injustive through the Lense of Mice and Men

Poverty
Workers live with very little and struggle to survive.
Migration
George and Lennie reflect real migrant workers, especially those affected by the Dust Bowl.
Labor
Jobs are temporary, low-paying, and offer no protection.
Injustice
The novel highlights racism, sexism, and the mistreatment of vulnerable people.

Literature's Impact on America's Understanding of the Era

Steinbeck’s work helped people understand the Great Depression on a personal level, building empathy and showing the human cost behind the economic crisis.
