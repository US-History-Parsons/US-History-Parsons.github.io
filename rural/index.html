Impact of the Great Depression on Rural Life



Falling Farm Prices

Crop prices dropped sharply, so farmers earned very little even when they produced large amounts of food.

Debt and Foreclosures

Many farmers couldn’t repay loans on land and equipment, leading banks to take their farms.

Overproduction

Farmers kept producing more crops to try to make money, but this oversupply drove prices even lower.

Dust Bowl Migration

Severe droughts and dust storms during the Dust Bowl forced many farming families to leave their land and migrate west, especially to California, in search of work.

Tenant Farmers and Sharecroppers

These workers were especially vulnerable since they didn’t own land; many lost jobs and homes when landowners cut costs or lost their farms.
