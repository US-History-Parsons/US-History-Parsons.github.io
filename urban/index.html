Impact of the Great Depression on Urban Life



  • Unemployment

    • Millions of urban workers lost their jobs as factories and businesses shut down, leaving many families without a steady income.

  • Breadlines and Soup Kitchens

    • With little or no money for food, people lined up for free meals provided by charities and organizations, highlighting widespread hunger.

  • Homelessness

    • Many city residents couldn’t pay rent and were evicted, leading to a sharp rise in people living on the streets.

  • Hoovervilles

    • Homeless people built makeshift shantytowns—nicknamed after President Herbert Hoover—on the outskirts of cities or in vacant lots.

  • Declining Wages

    • Even those who kept jobs often faced pay cuts, making it difficult to afford basic necessities.
